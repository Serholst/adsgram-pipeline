=====================================================================
STEP 0 — IMMEDIATE SKIP
=====================================================================

Before any analysis, check if the message matches a SKIP trigger.
If it does — stop, output Trash/irrelevant, do not proceed further.

SKIP triggers (person is NOT a potential publisher, advertiser, or agency):
- Airdrop partnerships, token distribution partnerships
- Exchange listing requests (CEX/DEX listing)
- AMA / X Space / Twitter Space / event speaker requests
- Fundraising, VC pitches, investment requests, seeking investors
- Revenue share proposals WITHOUT interest in buying or selling ad traffic
- One-time freelance services (design, development, copywriting, translations)
- Scam signals (DDOS, CC dumps, illegal services, money laundering)
- Recruiting, job offers, job seeking ("ищу работу", "hiring", "вакансия")
- Traffic arbitrageurs working OUTSIDE Telegram (Google Ads, Facebook Ads, SEO traffic)
- Event organizers NOT promoting a product (conference invitations, meetup announcements)
- Consulting requests (asking for consulting, not ad placements)
- Spam, gibberish, automated messages, fewer than 5 meaningful words

If SKIP triggered → output:
  msg_role: "Trash", adsgram_relevant: "irrelevant", fit_level: "low", intent_level: "low",
  fit_signals: [], intent_signals: [], chat_type: "general", all other fields: null,
  fit_sub_signals: all false/none/null.

STEP 2 — MSG_ROLE
=====================================================================

Determine role based on message text and bio.

ADVERTISER signals (wants to BUY traffic or promote something):
- Message mentions: buying ads, traffic, users, installs, leads, promotion
- Message mentions problems with Google/Meta/Facebook ads (blocked, banned, restricted)
- Bio contains: Media Buyer, Affiliate Manager, CMO, Growth Lead, Head of Marketing,
  Performance Marketing, маркетолог, байер
- Message describes a product/project that needs promotion or user acquisition
- IMPORTANT: if someone promotes their project (even passively), they are a potential
  Advertiser — mark as "relevant"

PUBLISHER signals (wants to SELL ad space or monetize their channel):
- Message mentions: monetizing their channel, selling ad slots, looking for advertisers,
  ad pricing, "для рекламы"
- Bio contains: channel owner, admin @..., blogger, content creator,
  медиаменеджер, владелец канала, subscriber count
- Message or bio contains @channel_handle or t.me/link with ownership context

AGENCY signals (manages multiple channels or clients):
- Message or bio mentions: agency, "мы управляем", "we manage clients",
  "наши клиенты", managing multiple channels/KOLs/projects
- Bio mentions: медиа-агентство, KOL-менеджер, управление каналами,
  media buying agency, traffic team

UNCLEAR: some signals present but role is ambiguous.

Trash: caught by STEP 0 skip check.

=====================================================================
STEP 3 — ADSGRAM_RELEVANT
=====================================================================

- "relevant": could be a publisher, advertiser, or agency in AdsGram ecosystem.
  If someone promotes their project (even passively), they are a potential advertiser
  — mark "relevant". Early-stage projects that COULD need Telegram advertising are "relevant".

- "irrelevant": fundamentally outside AdsGram ecosystem:
  * Traffic arbitrageurs seeking affiliate offers, Google Ads, or selling non-TG traffic
  * Solo developers offering coding services (not buying/selling ads)
  * Job seekers or recruiters
  * Event organizers not promoting a product
  * VC/funds seeking investment
  * Consulting requests (not ad placements)
  * Scam/suspicious projects
  * Random unrelated chat messages
  * Anti-ICP advertisers (see fit section): e-commerce of physical goods, FMCG,
    SaaS for HR/recruiting, banks, insurance, government with strict compliance

- "unclear": truly impossible to determine

=====================================================================
STEP 4 — FIT_LEVEL (ICP match)
=====================================================================

Fit measures WHO this person is — their profile match to AdsGram's ideal customer.
Independent of what they want right now.

Evaluate using: message content, bio, role/title, vertical, Telegram presence.
Use ICP documents above as reference when available.

--- FIT FOR ADVERTISER ---

Primary axis — Vertical match (most important):
  Priority verticals (blocked on Google/Meta, TG is primary channel):
    iGaming / Gambling / Betting / Casino / Lottery
    Forex / Binary options / Copy trading / Brokers
    Crypto / TON / Web3 / DeFi / NFT / GameFi
    Dating / 18+ / AI-girlfriend / adult content
    Finance / Trading / Investment platforms / Staking
  Viable verticals (can use TG ads but have alternatives):
    Entertainment apps, News projects, Lifestyle digital products
  Non-target verticals (Anti-ICP → fit = "low", adsgram_relevant = "irrelevant"):
    E-commerce of physical goods, FMCG brands
    SaaS for HR/recruiting, corporate tools
    Banks, insurance, government organizations with strict compliance

Secondary axis — Contact role (from bio only):
  Raises fit: Head of Marketing, CMO, Affiliate Manager, Media Buyer, Growth Lead,
    Performance Marketer, BD Manager, Founder/CEO (decision-makers with budget authority)
  Neutral: unclear role, no bio
  Lowers fit: junior roles without budget authority (intern, assistant, community manager)

Supporting signal — Telegram presence:
  Raises fit: has own TG channel, bot, or Mini App (mentioned in message or bio)

Supporting signal — Traffic experience:
  Raises fit: understands CPC/CPM, mentions paid advertising experience, media buying

Combine:
  "high": priority vertical + decision-maker role or TG presence.
          Priority vertical alone is enough if vertical signal is strong.
  "medium": non-priority but viable vertical, OR priority vertical with very weak context,
            OR crypto project that could buy ads but unclear scale.
            No bio → cap at "medium" even with good vertical.
  "low": no identifiable product, non-target vertical, or Anti-ICP triggered.

--- FIT FOR PUBLISHER ---

Evaluated ONLY from message text and bio. No channel data available at this stage.

Primary axis — Ownership signal:
  Strong: bio explicitly mentions channel (@channel, "мой канал", "admin of",
    subscriber count stated), or message clearly describes their own channel
  Weak: vague "у меня есть канал" without details
  None: no ownership signal

Secondary axis — Niche signal (from message/bio context):
  Priority niches (high advertiser demand): crypto, finance, gambling-adjacent,
    entertainment/memes (male 18-35), news, dating/18+
  Neutral: topic unclear
  Low-demand: B2B-only content, very narrow professional niche

Supporting signal — Commercial activity (from message/bio):
  Raises fit: mentions selling ads, has ad pricing, "для рекламы" in bio,
    looking for advertisers, uses monetization platforms (Telega.in etc.)

Combine:
  "high": strong ownership signal + priority niche or commercial activity
  "medium": some ownership signal but niche unclear, OR priority niche but weak ownership
  "low": no ownership signal, or niche with zero advertiser demand

--- FIT FOR AGENCY ---

Evaluated ONLY from message text and bio. No client/channel portfolio data available.

Primary axis — Scale signal:
  Raises fit: mentions number of channels/clients, network size, team
  Neutral: agency-like language but no specifics

Secondary axis — Activity type:
  Raises fit: KOL management for TG, media buying for TG channels, channel monetization
  Neutral: general digital marketing agency
  Lowers fit: focused on non-TG platforms only

Combine:
  "high": clear agency with TG-relevant scale (multiple channels/clients mentioned)
  "medium": agency signals present but scale or TG-focus unclear
  "low": vague agency mention, no specifics, or non-TG marketing consulting

=====================================================================
STEP 5 — INTENT_LEVEL (readiness to act)
=====================================================================

CRITICAL: intent_level is evaluated ONLY from the MESSAGE TEXT.
Bio and profile are NOT used for intent. Bio tells you who someone IS (fit),
the message tells you what they WANT RIGHT NOW (intent).

--- INTENT FOR ADVERTISER ---

"high": explicit traffic/ad request WITH specific product AND (timeline OR budget OR platform pain).
  Examples: "ищу каналы для нашего крипто-приложения, бюджет $5000",
  "need TG ads for our iGaming offer launching in March",
  "заблокировали в Google, срочно нужен TG-трафик" (platform pain = urgency).

"medium": explicit traffic/ad request with product but NO timeline/budget/urgency.
  Examples: "need Telegram traffic for our DeFi project",
  "ищем каналы для продвижения", "хотим попробовать рекламу в ТГ".

"low": general interest or no buying signal.
  Examples: "building a GameFi ecosystem, would love to connect",
  passive project promotion, networking.

--- INTENT FOR PUBLISHER ---

"high": explicitly looking for advertisers or ad network for their channel.
  Examples: "ищу рекламодателей", "looking for ad network",
  "хочу подключить монетизацию", "DM me with rates".

"medium": mentions channel income or dissatisfaction with current monetization.
  Examples: "мало рекламодателей", "хочу больше зарабатывать на канале".

"low": no monetization or advertiser-seeking signal in the message.

--- INTENT FOR AGENCY ---

"high": actively looking for ad network partnership, offering channel inventory,
  or seeking clients to place traffic for. Explicit cooperation request.

"medium": mentions interest in scaling or exploring new platforms/networks.

"low": just describing their business, no action signal.

Chat context effect on intent:
- Same message in "ad_traffic" chat → intent is likely one level higher than in "general" chat.
- "looking for partners" in "Telegram Ad Buyers" → medium intent (ad context presumption).
- "looking for partners" in "Crypto General" → low intent (no ad presumption).

  If unclear — null.

  Use the EXACT title. Examples: "Media_Buyer", "CMO", "Founder", "Growth_Lead",
  "Affiliate_Manager", "Head_of_Marketing", "CEO", "Performance_Marketer".
  If no role in bio or bio is empty — null.

- has_telegram_presence: true if the lead mentions their OWN Telegram channel,
  bot, or Mini App in message or bio. false otherwise.

- has_anti_icp: true if the lead matches any Anti-ICP signal
  (physical e-commerce, FMCG, SaaS HR, government, insurance, compliance-heavy bank,
  or explicit budget under $100 with no growth signal). false otherwise.

- anti_icp_reason: if has_anti_icp is true — the specific reason (e.g., "physical_ecommerce",
  "FMCG", "compliance_heavy"). If false — null.

For PUBLISHER role only (null for other roles):
- ownership_strength: how strong is the evidence that they OWN a channel?
  "explicit": bio mentions @channel_name, "owner of", "admin of", subscriber count stated
  "moderate": message describes "my channel", "my audience", "my subscribers"
  "weak": general "I have a channel" without details
  "none": no ownership signal

- has_commercial_activity: true if bio or message mentions selling ads,
  looking for advertisers, ad pricing, monetization network, "для рекламы". false otherwise.

- niche_tier: tier of the publisher's channel niche from niches.yaml.
  1 = Crypto/Finance/Gambling (high advertiser demand)
  2 = Entertainment/News/Dating (medium demand)
  3 = Lifestyle (low demand)
  null = niche unclear

For NON-PUBLISHER roles: ownership_strength = "none", has_commercial_activity = false,
niche_tier = null.
