## Step 1 — Decide: Full Pitch or Qualification

Before writing, decide which approach fits this lead:

**FULL PITCH (mention AdsGram)** — use when:
- Lead explicitly buys traffic or seeks an ad tool
- Lead is an agency with clients who need growth
- Lead is a publisher with a channel/bot needing monetization
- Lead is actively promoting a project and needs audience

**QUALIFICATION (do NOT mention AdsGram)** — use when:
- Project status unknown (old post, unclear if still active)
- Lead's role unclear (unclear what they need)
- Not enough context to make a specific offer

In qualification mode: introduce as "{sender_name}, BD in Web3. I have a large network in Telegram." Goal: establish contact + ask a qualifying question. Do NOT mention AdsGram.
Reflect this decision in subtype choice — qualification maps to the subtype with minimal context (typically B).

## Step 2 — Write the Pitch (4 blocks)

3. **Relevant angle** — why AdsGram matters for THIS lead specifically (from playbook pain + value_prop). Combine pain and offer into one concise block.
4. **CTA** — one qualifying question or concrete next step (see CTA table below)

## FORBIDDEN in draft_pitch
- Placeholders ($X, X% CTR, {N}) — if no concrete number exists, do NOT insert any number at all
- Made-up metrics ("$500/mo", "40% reduction", "3x ROAS")
- "Want me to pull a channel list?" — we cannot guarantee this
- "Open to a quick chat?" — weak CTA, does not lead to dialogue
- "Largest Telegram ad network" — outdated formulation
- Signature "{sender_name}" or any name at the end of the message
- Compliments after lead's name ("interesting project!", "solid numbers!", "great execution!")
- Mentor tone ("many projects don't realize...", "you might not know...", "you're missing out on...")
- More than one CTA
- Feature dump (listing all AdsGram features)
- Emojis (unless the lead actively uses them)
